@TMPL(expl_header.txt)@ Loss burst measurement

About this test: Packet loss, where a message is sent but not received, occurs continuously in the network. Normally, this is not a problem if the loss rate is low. But what often occurs, especially with wireless network, is bursts of loss: the network seems good, but drops in and out.

This test is run in the background during the applet's execution, continuously monitoring the state of the network and recording when packets do not arrive, to check for such problems.

What if this test reports a problem: If you are on a wireless network, the most likely cause is random interference and noise. If you move closer to the base station you will probably experience better connectivity. If you are on a wired network, this may be a problem with your connection to the Internet and you should contact your ISP for assistance.
