About this test: Home network gateways typically translate the local IP addresses of computers on your network to a single global IP address that is used to route your traffic across the Internet. Such network address translation (NAT) allows you to share Internet connectivity in your network and reduces the overall number of routable IP addresses in use, but complicates many applications.

In this test, we determine whether your machine sits behind a NAT. We also determine aspects of how the actual translation is done; in particular, we look at the sequencing of the TCP source ports of subsequent connections crossing the NAT. If you are not behind a NAT, this test instead shows how your machine allocates TCP source ports.
